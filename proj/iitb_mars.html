The IITB Mars Rover Project

2015

Head of Autonomous Subsystem (Sept. ‘15 - Jan. ‘18)

The IITB Mars Rover project is a student initiative at IIT Bombay to build a prototype Mars rover capable of extra-terrestrial robotics and to participate in the University Rover Challenge at the Mars Society’s Mars Desert Research Station, Utah. I serve as the head of the Computer Vision & Navigation subsystem, responsible for the autonomous operations of the robot, and maneuvering when contact with base station is lost. I have also been associated with the GUI subsystem to ensure robust simulation, visualization and interfacing of the base station with the mobile rover.


In the latest edition, we plan to have a full-stack SLAM implementation with visual odometry and GPS feedback. The team participated in the 2018 edition of the University Rover Challenge, Utah. Below are videos of our design reviews for the 2017 and 2018 editions of the competition, which summarise the work done by our team.
