Robust Localization of an Arbitrary Distribution of Radioactive Sources for Aerial Inspection

2018

Dhruv Shah 1 and Sebastian Scherer 2

1 Department of Electrical Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology Bombay
2 Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University

WM2018 Conference, March 18-27, 2018, Phoenix, Arizona, USA

[Poster] [BibTeX]

Radioactive waste, even after years of isolation and containment, can be hazardous. Developing autonomous systems for work in the nuclear complex, including decommissioned and contaminated nuclear sites, is an active subject of research in robotics. Despite the existence of gamma imaging cameras, their weight (2-4 kgs.) and exposure time (2-10 min.) makes them impractical for mounting on most aerial robots. In this research, we present a novel algorithm for the robust localization of an arbitrary distribution of radiation sources using bilayer Sequential Monte Carlo (bi-SMC) methods using light-weight flux detectors. We achieve near-perfect accuracy, in terms of F1-scores (> 0.95), while allowing the algorithm to scale, both to large regions in space, and to higher dimensional spaces.

  • Our work focuses on using particle flux measurements from cheap, light-weight detectors like the Teviso RD3024, ground truth odometry and visual cues to localize radiation sources using a hybrid formulation of a particle filter and clustering techniques. The proposed method is capable of
    • Resolving large number of sources efficiently
    • Scaling to large regions in space (large nuclear sites/tunnels, for example)
    • Localizing bulk radiation sources
  • The results of extensive simulation experiments are compared against existing work by Chin et al. (2011) and the proposed algorithm fares better in terms of localization accuracy for large number of sources and run-time.
  • Experiments were conducted in real world to validate the robustness of the algorithm to motion and measurement uncertainties.
